Common questions

School & institutional grounds - common questions

Do you hold a Working with Children Check?
Yes. Kanga Gardening holds a Victorian Working with Children Check (WWCC) and a police check, and is a White Card holder. Because the same person services the site every visit, your sign-in register shows one known, checked operator, not a rotating crew.
Can you work outside school hours?
Yes. Visits are scheduled with the office to suit the site: early starts, after hours, or during term breaks. Noisier work like mowing and blowing is timed away from class time and pick-up, and term holidays are used for bigger catch-up visits.
Are you insured to work on school and community sites?
Yes. Kanga Gardening carries $20M public and products liability and is fully insured. A Certificate of Currency and a safe work method statement (SWMS) are available on request for your compliance file, and site inductions are no problem.
What does school grounds maintenance include?
Recurring mowing of ovals, lawns and play spaces (ride-on and push), hedging and pruning with paths and sightlines kept clear, garden-bed maintenance and weed control, and green-waste removal every visit, under one fixed fee, with seasonal condition reporting.
How is weed control handled around children?
Carefully. Spraying is planned with the site, typically during term breaks or outside operating hours, and carried out strictly to label directions, and the treated areas are flagged with the office so staff know what was done and where. Seasonal condition reports record it for your file.
