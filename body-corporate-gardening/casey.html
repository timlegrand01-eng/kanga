Body Corporate · City of Casey

For Casey's new-estate owners corporations

Casey is one of the fastest-growing municipalities in the country, and that means a constant stream of new master-planned estates — each with a freshly-formed owners corporation responsible for landscaped common areas, retained gardens, entry features and shared open space. Once the developer's maintenance period ends, that upkeep lands on the OC, and it needs a contractor who'll keep the brand-new landscaping looking the way it did at handover.

The newer plantings across Cranbourne, Clyde and Clyde North are still establishing, so consistent weeding, watering-in and feeding through the first seasons makes a real difference. The local soils are heavy reactive clay that cracks in summer and waterlogs in winter — tough on lawns and beds — so Kanga matches the schedule to the season and keeps green-waste removal and service reports included as standard.
