Why we put this in writing

Five promises, in writing, for every site

Anyone can say they're reliable. The Kanga Standard sets out what that actually means on your site: how we present and conduct ourselves, how we schedule, how we report, how we communicate, and how we keep the work safe and compliant. It's the same standard on every property, whether it's a small complex or a large commercial site, from your first enquiry to the last invoice.

The five promises

What you can expect, every visit

1 · Presentation & conduct on site

Turned out properly and working safely around residents, staff and the public. Gates left as found, pets and access respected, noise kept to sensible hours, and the site left clean, with clippings and prunings cleared every visit, not left for someone else.

2 · Reliability

A set schedule that suits the property, and we attend on the day we say we will. If weather forces a change, you hear it from us, so you're not left wondering. The same operator services your site, so nothing has to be re-explained each time.

3 · Reporting

A short service report after each visit: what was done, and anything worth flagging: a failing tree, an irrigation fault, a safety issue. So you can show residents the levies are working, and you're never chasing confirmation that we came.

4 · Communication

One point of contact, the owner, and a reply to enquiries usually within one business day. No call-centre, no ticket queue, no being passed around. If something needs a decision, you get a straight answer. And after your first month, we check in and ask what we could be doing better.

5 · Safety & compliance

$20M public and products liability, fully insured, police-checked, WWCC and White Card carded. Safe work methods on site, safe chemical handling for weed control, and green waste taken away and disposed of responsibly. Certificate of Currency and a safe work method statement available on request.

And one operator behind all of it

Every promise above is easier to keep because the person who quotes your site is the person who maintains it. Accountability doesn't get diluted across a rotating crew. It stops with the owner, Tim Le Grand.

Proof, not promises

What our reporting actually looks like

Promise three says you'll never chase confirmation that we came. In practice, that's a short service note after each visit: what was done, and anything worth flagging before it becomes a cost.

Our larger commercial sites also receive a seasonal condition report like the one shown here: a plain-English, area-by-area summary of how the grounds are tracking, with photo records and clear status calls. Green means healthy and on track. Amber means we've spotted something and we're watching it.

It's the difference between paying an invoice and knowing exactly what you paid for, and it gives a committee or facilities manager something concrete to table at the next meeting.
